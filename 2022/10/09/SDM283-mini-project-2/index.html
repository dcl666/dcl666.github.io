Introduction

In this project, we mainly use the knowledge of material mechanics to design a workbench for placing eight 3D printers(Raise3D E2).

Material related specification constraints

Overall analysis

  1. Intensity

    $$
    min(Load)=8*35=280kg
    $$

    Therefore, the minimum strength of the supporting structure shall ensure that no obvious deformation will occur when subjected to min Load, which is sufficient for a long time.

  2. Deflection

    $$
    max(Deflection)=0.1mm
    $$

    The 3D printer has a leveling process before use. If the plane it is placed on is not horizontal, it will cause leveling errors; At the same time, too much inclination of the plane during printing may cause the initial support to slip, resulting in poor print quality or even failure.

    After asking about the specific information of this printer, we got the allowable inclination of 0.1mm, so the maximum deformation of the overall structure is 0.1mm, and the smaller the better.

  3. Weight

    $$
    max(weight)=1140kg
    $$

    The alloy steel with high strength is selected as the initial material to ensure that it absolutely meets the strength requirements, but the total mass of the structure is indeed too large. Therefore, after the stress analysis, the material will be iterated to minimize the structure.

  4. Size

    Because the placement method is 4 × 2. Therefore, in addition to the length of the printer itself, the middle spacing (considering the simultaneous opening of the doors at both ends for placing printing materials) and the length from the edge should also be considered.

    $$
    Length:（61+23×2）× 4+3×10+5×2=468cm
    $$

    Because the placement method is 4 × 2. Therefore, in addition to the width of the printer itself, the middle space should also be considered (considering that the top cover is opened at the same time, it is found that the top cover is not more than the width of the bottom as a whole when it is opened to the maximum extent, so no additional increase is required), And the length from the edge (after the design investigation, it is found that the existing design is that the printer exceeds the table edge, which should make the chassis in the printer easy to reposition after being pulled out, so this small design is continued).

    $$
    Width:60× 2+11×2-5×2 + 5 =137cm
    $$

    Because the placement method is 4 × 2. Therefore, it is the height of the bottom support plus the height of a printer. But at this time, the overall height needs to be determined according to ergonomics, and then subtract the height of the printer to get the height of the bottom support. According to the average height of men and women, the overall height is within the range of [160, 170]. The height of the printer includes two parts - the height of the printer itself and the increased height of the top cover opening, which is about 98.5cm after measurement. Therefore, the height interval of the bottom support structure is [61.5, 71.5].

Stress concentration analysis

  1. Long pole of frame structure

(1) Part function: support and fix the upper table, and connect the table with the main support structure;

(2) Stress concentration part: the part contacting the lower diagonal rod;

(3) Cause of stress concentration

The rod is rigidly connected with the lower diagonal tie rod, and the contact area of the connecting part is small, the stress is large, and the stress difference between the connecting part and the adjacent section is large, so the stress concentration phenomenon appears.

(4) Improvement measures

Increase the contact area between the lower diagonal tie rod and the member, extend the diagonal tie rod and move its connection with the member to the middle of the member as far as possible to reduce local stress.

  1. Support rod of frame structure

(1) Part function: support the upper table, connect and fix the main body of the frame structure;

(2) Stress concentration part: near the contact with the side diagonal tie rod fastener;

(3) Cause of stress concentration

The rod is rigidly connected with the side diagonal tie rod fixing, and the force acting on the nearby area becomes larger due to the extrusion from the fixing, so the stress concentration phenomenon occurs.

(4) Improvement measures

Increase the cross-sectional area of the member and adopt smooth transition to reduce local stress;

Materials with slightly lower rigidity after surface strengthening (shot peening, rolling, nitriding, etc.) are selected to enhance their fatigue resistance.

  1. Diagonal tie rod of frame structure

(1) Part function: support the upper table, connect and fix the frame structure to make it more stable;

(2) Stress concentration part: near the contact part with the table;

(3) Cause of stress concentration

The contact area between the bar and the table is too small, and the contact position with the table is just the force bearing position of the table, so the force on the bar is also large, which leads to a large stress on this part and a large difference between the stress on its side. The stress concentration is caused.

(4) Improvement measures

Cut off the sharp corner above the diagonal tie rod to avoid contact with the upper table.

  1. Frame structure diagonal tie rod fastener

(1) Part function: connect and fix the frame structure;

(2) Stress concentration part: near the bolt hole;

(3) Cause of stress concentration

Holes are left on the fastener, reducing its cross-sectional area, and the bolt used for fixing in the hole has a large reaction force near its hole due to large force. However, the part of the fastener far from the hole bears less stress, has a large cross-sectional area, and has small stress, so stress concentration occurs.

(4) Improvement measures

A certain thickness of material shall be excavated at the upper and lower sides of the hole to reduce the rigidity of this part, so as to ease the stress concentration.

Failure Analysis

The simulation function of solidworks software is used for static stress analysis to simulate the stress and deformation of the designed table for placing printers under the load of eight RAISE E2 printers.

Without bottom support structure

  1. Load

    According to our design, apply a load of 400N on each groove surface where the printer is placed, and fix the four legs of the table under ideal conditions.

  2. Stress analysis

    It can be seen from the stress simulation results that although the load is directly applied to the wooden plane of the table, the stress on the wooden plane is relatively small because of the large area of load application, and the stress on the wooden plane is almost the smallest in the whole structure, about $8.253*10 ^ 2 Pa.$

    Because the contact area between the frame at the bottom and the wood plane is relatively small, and the weight of the wood plane is added to the load on the frame based on the quality of the printer, the stress on the frame will be much larger than that on the wood plane, and even the part of the whole structure with the largest stress, about $1.847 * 10 ^ 7 Pa.$

  3. Displayment analysis

    In the same static stress analysis example, we can get the deformation of the table under the same fixed and load as the stress analysis:

    Through the simulation results, we can find that the most deformed part of the model is in the center of the wood plane. Because the wood plane only receives the supporting force on its four sides, the center lacks the supporting force applied from the bottom, but because of the placement of the printer, the load applied to the center of the wood plane is relatively large, so the center of the wood plane will have a large deformation, up to 2.31mm. Even at the position where the printer is placed, the deformation reaches 1mm, but the maximum allowable plane deviation of the printer itself is 0.1mm, so the deformation cannot meet the requirements. The reason for this result may be that the elastic modulus of the selected material itself is small, and no bottom support is applied to the center of the wood plane. Therefore, in order to solve this problem, we plan to apply the bottom support in the place where the printer is placed on the wooden plane.

    Similarly, we can also obtain the structural strain image after simulation:

    Because the material we choose to simulate is alloy steel, which has a large elastic modulus, the resulting strain is less than 0.01.

  4. Buckling analysis

    Because when the applied load is too large, the structure may buckle, resulting in some structural failure. Through buckling analysis, find out the position where buckling occurs, and observe the stress change after buckling.

    Fix the four legs of the model without bottom support, apply a load of 100N on the position where the printer is placed, and calculate the load factor at this time through the buckling analysis of solidworks software.

    It can be seen from the simulation results that the load factor is 123.41. In the simulation of solidworks, different levels of grid refinement of the structure will affect the results of the load factor obtained from the buckling analysis, so the results obtained from the simulation will also be biased and may not be correct.

    We multiply the applied load by the load factor as a new load. In the static stress analysis, we can obtain the static stress analysis results of the bottom support structure during buckling by the same fixing method and load application method.

    At this time, it can be seen from the simulation results that the maximum deformation has reached 76.28mm, and the entire structure has undergone visible deformation. At the same time, this deformation is far greater than the printer’s requirement of 0.01mm, which will cause the printer to fail.

Iteration

Material

In order to ensure the stability and strength of the structure in the initial design, alloy steel with large strength and weight was selected as the material of the external frame and bottom support. No specific analysis was carried out for each structure. The data obtained from the previous stress analysis can be used to re select materials to meet the requirements of lightweight

In terms of material selection, we iterated to reduce the mass and ensure that the deformation does not exceed the specified value. By selecting materials with small density and specific modulus within the required range of modulus, lightweight can be carried out.

In the support at the lower end, considering the mass of alloy steel and carbon steel previously selected is large, aluminum alloy is selected by searching for materials with smaller density. The common aluminum alloy material is 1060.4032. 1060 is cheaper, 4032 has higher silicon content, and is better than 1060 in density and modulus. Therefore, 4032 is selected for larger parts and 1060 is selected for smaller parts. Apply them to the four outermost brackets and the eight brackets at the lower end of the printer.

The simulation analysis of the bottom support structure after iterative optimization is shown in the figure:

Its mass is optimized to 14 kg per group.

At the same time, common desktop materials are searched, which are often used as particleboard and medium density fiberboard. Its modulus and density are similar to that of light wood. We also tried to apply it to marble, but we measured that the mass of the table top when applying marble was 426 kg, which was far beyond our expectations for mass, so we rejected it.

The simulation results of the overall desktop are shown in the figure:

Its mass is optimized to 80 kg.

Analysis after iteration

  1. Load analysis

    Basically, the load size and direction on all structures have not changed, but the change of materials and specific structures has led to a slight change in the stress. At the same time, the deformation size has changed, and the stress concentration has decreased.

  2. Stress analysis

    For outer frame and table plate, the overall distribution is more uniform, and the maximum value is decreased from $1.847 × 10^7$ to $1.614 × 10^7$, the minimum value is increased from $8.253 × 10^2$ to $1.283 × 10^3$.

    For bottom support structure, the overall distribution is more uniform, and the maximum value is decreased from $2.652 × 10^5$to $2.103 × 10^5$, the minimum value is increased from $3.094 × 10^2$ to $4.505× 10^2$.

    In general, the overall change of stress is small, and the structure can still meet the strength requirements.

  3. Strain analysis

    For outer frame and table plate, except for the minimum deformation, other deformation increases to a small extent, but the basic deformation of the external frame is 0.001 mm, which meets the requirements. As for the large deformation in the middle of the upper table, the deformation will be greatly reduced after the installation of the bottom support structure.

    For bottom support structure, except for the minimum deformation, the other deformation is basically 3 times of the previous deformation, but the basic deformation is far less than 0.01 mm, meeting the requirements; Even if it is loaded with one more serving board (with a small mass), it will not change too much.

  4. Buckling analysis

    For outer frame and table plate, the overall amplitude increases slightly, and the load factor decreases significantly from 123.41 to 58.159, that is, the maximum load decreases significantly, which makes it easier to fail.

    For bottom support structure, the overall amplitude increased slightly, and the load factor decreased significantly from 6672.2 to 1603.9, that is, the maximum load decreased significantly, making it easier to fail.

Conclusion

After secondary design, a relatively satisfactory supporting structure is finally obtained, which is divided into three parts: the upper table board, the outer frame, and the bottom supporting structure(× 8).
