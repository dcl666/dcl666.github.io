Introduction

This project focused on analysis of motion patterns of multi-degree-of-freedom robotic arms, as well as simulation of actual motion by motion simulation software(Adams) and simulation of motion trajectories using programming(Matlab).

Tasks are below:

Procedure:

Initial model analysis part

Firstly, for the ggb model analysis, the basic motion pattern of RGB460 is obtained:

The model can be divided into three parallelograms - when the bottom point C is driven by point 0, the FG rod is the main motion rod and points D and D’ do not move (representing the control of up and down motion); when the upper point D is driven by point O, the model moves as a whole with the same relative position (representing the back and forth motion) .
These two motions are driven by different joints and are not equivalent (this is to be reflected in the simulation); meanwhile the BO bar is always in the same position; the motion can be controlled by changing the size of the two parallelogram angles on the left side (which can represent variables in matlab, i.e. angle change values).
Finally by some fixed values (rod length, angle) and variables can be based on the coordinates of the O point to represent the coordinates of all the remaining points, for the convenience of the subsequent programming.
