Introduction

In this project, based on a four wheel racing model, we use Adams to simulate its traveling state on bumpy roads, and change its own shock absorber to achieve the minimum vibration and deflection of the front beam.

Simple Research on Automobile Shock Absorber

Definition and basic functions

The shock absorber is one of the important components in the vehicle suspension system, which has an important impact on the vehicle driving safety and comfort.

There are two basic functions - correcting the transmission from uneven road surface to vehicle by adjusting vehicle body vibration; Quickly attenuate the wheel and axle vibration caused by road impact to provide a safe grip for the vehicle.

Suspension parameters

The main parameters related to the shock absorber are stiffness and damping coefficient, which have a great impact on the vehicle’s ride comfort. A reasonable matching of stiffness and damping coefficient can reduce the transfer function and improve the vehicle’s ride comfort - that is, reduce the free deflection at the end of the beam.

Damping coefficient

When the stiffness of the front and rear shock absorbers changes, the root mean square value of the total weighted acceleration at the seat changes, and the front damping change has less impact on the vehicle ride comfort than the rear damping. The higher the damping force, the more sporty the car is; the lower the damping force, the better the car’s comfort (definition: low speed level of vibration occurring on the whole vehicle and vehicle parts).

It can be seen from the figure that the amplification coefficient of body acceleration increases with the increase of damping constant in the frequency range between body and wheel resonance frequencies, and the comfort of the vehicle becomes worse.

There are three important characteristic curves in the research of shock absorber: damping force displacement curve, quasi-static and dynamic damping force velocity curve

The damping coefficient and attenuation coefficient describe the trend of the quasi-static damping force velocity characteristic curve. The damping coefficient affects the slope of the curve, while the attenuation index affects the concave convex trend of the curve.

Dynamic damping shows the relationship between damping force and piston speed at a certain excitation frequency - the overall trend is to decrease with the increase of excitation frequency.

Stiffness

When the front and rear spring stiffness changes, the root mean square value of the total weighted acceleration at the seat has a significant change, and the change of the front spring stiffness has less impact on the vehicle ride comfort than the change of the rear spring stiffness.

For flexible beam, we need to use flex function in Adams to construct it:

More information about rigid and flexible in stimulation software:

Most simulation analysis uses rigid components, which will not deform under the force. In reality, it can meet the requirements to treat most components as rigid bodies, because the elastic deformation between parts has little impact on the dynamic characteristics of each part of the mechanism.

However, it is necessary to consider the deformation of components, which will affect the accuracy results. When it is necessary to study the size and distribution of stress and load output of components, as well as thin-walled components and high-precision instrument components, they should be treated as flexible bodies, so that the calculation results will be more accurate. For flexible body mechanism, deformation plays a decisive role in dynamic influence.

Wheel Motion Mode

The motion part is the synchronous motion of four wheels - the speed remains the same and the size remains the same, which is $720^0/sec$.

Determination of simulation time

Since the purpose of simulation is to get the deflection of the beam carried by the car on the undulating road, reducing the time spent on the flat road and the time spent on falling can better meet the calculation and simulation requirements.

After several simulation experiments, 7.25s was selected as the total simulation time. The position of the car is as shown in the figure above - the car left the undulating road and did not start to fall, which is more in line with expectations.

Specific simulation operation - optimization design

Use design variable in Adams for parametric modeling, hoping to optimize the design.

The design variable is used in some scenes of adams, which can make the operation more convenient. For example, some groups of forces have the same size, so that they all reference the same design variable. Just change the value of the design variable, you can change all force sizes.

In this project, we use design variable for K/C(stiffness and damping coefficient).

Matlab stimulation

In the simulation, the damping system of the trolley is simplified as a spring with an elastic coefficient of k and a viscous damper with a viscosity coefficient of v. The damping system of the trolley shall ensure that there is no obvious boundary between passing and failing frequencies. Therefore, there should be a gradual transition in the design of the damping system. In the modeling of the system, the step signal is used to simulate the uneven road surface. If the step response of the trolley damping system presents a relatively large continuous oscillation, a flange on the road (equivalent to the step signal) will cause the trolley to bump.

Establishment of mathematical model of damping system

The model diagram of the damping system of the car can be shown in the above figure: $x (t)$ is the displacement of the wheel when driving relative to the horizontal road surface, and $y (t)$ is the displacement of the car chassis when driving relative to the horizontal road surface. Moreover, when $t=0$, the trolley is in balance on the horizontal road, that is, $x (t)=y (t)=0$. However, when the car moves on the uneven road, there will be $x (t) ≠ 0$, and the balance of the car will be broken.

Analysis of simulation results

  1. Effect of viscosity coefficient on damping characteristics

    From the measured k and m, we get $ω_ n=0.66$, let $ξ$= 0.1:0.1:1. Write a matlab program to call the model file for simulation, and draw the step response curve and bode diagram:

    Through the analysis of the image, it can be concluded that the damping system of the trolley is based on the natural frequency $ω_n$ to control, the value of $ω_n$ becomes larger, the more compressed the functions $h (t)$ and $s (t)$ as t in time, and the higher the oscillation frequency, so that the rise time of the system. If the natural frequency is low, the damping system tends to filter out slower changes, so as to provide a stable driving environment, but this will increase the rise time of the system, and the system will become sluggish.

    As the damping coefficient decreases, the cut-off curve of the system frequency response becomes steeper, and overshoot and oscillation tend to increase, which means that a compromise is found between the starting point and the frequency domain, that is, the system frequency and $ξ$ Is related to the value of. It can be seen from the figure $ξ= 0.4-0.8$, the adjustment time is relatively short, and a relatively stable driving environment can be obtained.

  2. Effect of elastic coefficient on damping characteristics

    At this time, instead of using the determined value of $\omega_n$, let $\omega_n = 1:1:10,ξ=0.7 $, compile a matlab program to call the model file for simulation, and draw the step response curve and bode diagram:

    It can be seen that the smaller the elastic coefficient is, the more sluggish the system’s response is. However, k is too large, the natural frequency is high, and the filtering in the damping system area changes rapidly, which leads to unstable driving of the car. Then the elastic coefficient can be a compromise value.
