SDM232 Project4——Linkage and cam

更新于2022-10-08|机械
|字数总计:693|阅读时长:4分钟|阅读量:

Introduction:

This project is to use GGB and Mech Designer to combine linkage and cam, then use Solidworks to produce a 3D model and use Solidworks motion to stimulate the motion.

At the designing stage of our linkage-cam, our group decided to use the knowledge we acquired to design a useful linkage-cam for our final project- the companion robot. We settled on the idea of a mechanical structure that can pour pills into a box in order to make up a prescription.

Procedure:

  • Geogebra

    The pouring linkage-cam requires two main functions. The first function is making the platform which holds the pills incline so that the pills can fall into the box. This is essentially a 2-positon synthesis which can be realized by linkages.

  • Mech Designer:

    The second function is controlling the pouring speed. To ensure the sufficiency of fill-up and pour-down time, we used cams to control the speed of rotation and leave some dwells.

  • Solidworks

    After generating the curve of our cam according to our motion timing requirements, we made a 3D model of tin Solidworks.

    (1) Input the trajectory of the cam which we generated in Mech Designer into Solidworks. (The trajectory transferred into Solidworks is generated according to 1000 points in Mech Designer).

    (2) Draw the 3D model of the cam according to the trajectory we just input.

    (3) Draw the four-bar-linkage according to the statistics (width, length, thickness) in GeoGebra model and Mech Design model.

    (4) Calculate and draw two bars to control the position relation between the driving bar of the linkage and the cam according to the position relation needed. Link two bars with the two edges of the driving bar of the linkage to form a triangle. Because the lengths of 3 sides of triangle are fixed. The position relation between the cam (vertex) and driving bar (opposite side of the vertex) is fixed.

    Detail of the two bars: One bar has a small cylinder hump on one edge which can fit into the trajectory so that the driving bar of the linkage can move along with the rotation of the cam.

    (5) Calculate and draw two bars to control the position relation between the static bar of the linkage and the cam. Link two bars with the two edges of the static bar of the linkage to form a triangle. Because the lengths of 3 sides of triangle are fixed. The center of the cam is now fixed. The cam can only rotate around the center axis of the cam.

    (6) Set the static bar of the four-bar-linkage as the foundation. Assemble the four-bar-linkage first, then fit the cam on the four-bar-linkage using other bars that are used to control the position relation between the cam and the linkage.

    (7) After assembling the pieces to form a linkage-cam model. Add a motion study on the model. Use a rotation motor to drive the cam and observe its motion.

Discussion and Conclusion

In the GeoGebra constructing process, we could not construct the structure following video. The structure we constructed always had more than one driving points. So we turned to others for help, learned a new technique, and solved the problem by adding a new drawing zone.
In MD PRO design process, we rebuilt the four-bar linkages based on the mechanism we designed in GeoGebra. But later when we added a motion controller and the MD PRO automatically generated a 2D cam, we got an odd-shape cam. Different lines overlapped with each other and it hard to make such cam. So we had to rearrange the position of the motion controller and adjust motion timing. Finally, the cam became the irregular circle which can satisfy our needs.

In SOLIDWORKS modeling process, the assembly in the model can move along the path as we designed. But in motion studies, it deviated from the original path and jump to another position.

Speculation: The trajectory transferred into Solidworks is generated according to 1000
points of the trajectory in Mech Design, insufficient statistics results in lack of precision and a failed motion study.
