SDM232 Project3——Linkage Design

更新于2022-10-14|机械
|字数总计:565|阅读时长:3分钟|阅读量:

Introduction:

The project is to design a giftware to put on top of a cup using linkage and Stirling engine. The Stirling engine is to provide power source for the product. We need to design a linkage mechanism to be driven by either a crank-rocker or a crank-slider four-bar mechanism.

Procedure:

  • Research

    We first researched on some basic linkage mechanisms. In this process, we found an interesting linkage structure called Peaucellier-Lipkin linkage.

    When we start to design our own mechanism, the first thing we need to do is calculate the position the hinges and the length of each rods: It is composed of two groups of rods, one group is composed of 4 short rods of the same length to form a rhombus with a variable shape, and the other group is composed of two long rods of equal length. Determine the specific size according to the ratio of the found video and enlarge it as a whole after a unified transformation. Regarding the structure of the connecting part, it needs to be calculated according to the actual size and the error of 3D printing. At the same time, it also needs to be compared and optimized continuously.

    We used a laser cutter and an acrylic sheet to make the first prototype of our design. And, of course, the ball in the mechanism we used a steel ball of the right size.

  • Assembly and testing:
    In our assembly process, we install the linkage mechanism by four different kinds of rods. They have suitable length and in the suitable place which we have calculated. Then we connect the end of the linkage mechanism to elevator and slide section (all the details are in the installation instruction of flapping-wing mechanism section).

Discussion and Conclusion

In our flapping-wing mechanism, the driven end of the linkage is to reciprocate in a straight vertical line. We use this mechanism to control the rolling of the ball, including stopping at the bottom of the slide and transporting the ball from the bottom of the slide to the top of the slide. In this process, all the power and energy come from the Stirling mechanism connected to the active end of the linkage. he connecting rods use energy generated by thermal collisions in the Stirling mechanism to transport and intercept the balls to the lowest end.
