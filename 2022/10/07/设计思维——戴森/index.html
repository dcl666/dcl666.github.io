SDM212 Dyson Case Study

5 stages of design thinking process

(1) Empathy
When James Dyson cleaned the house dust himself, he found that the vacuum cleaner would block the hole of the vacuum bag due to the absorption of dust, and the suction power would be greatly reduced, this creates a sense of frustration and empathy with other users to James Dyson.
(2) Define problem
The powder continues to block the tiny holes, resulting in a greatly reduced suction.
(3) Idea
Dyson designed a double cyclone vacuum cleaner. When the airflow moves quickly along a spiral path, all dirt particles will be thrown out of the airflow due to strong centrifugal force. In this way, the dirt can be separated from the air flow without using any type of filter, and the dirt is collected at the bottom of the cylinder.
(4) Prototype
The dust will be replaced in the cone in the middle, then centrifugal force will be used to rotate it to the edge of the tank, and then it will be removed from the tank. This is the first of 5127 prototypes.
Cooling fan (640 prototype products manufactured)
(5) Test
After completing the first generation of products, Dyson conducted market research: For Japanese, their houses are smaller and cleaner, and do not require very large machines; for Americans, they like large machines, so the production cost should be the primary consideration.
They focus on how the machines are assembled together and constantly test materials to achieve the goal of sustainable use of the machines

Types of prototyping methods

(1) Sketching
When designing the steering wheel, the designer directly draws the rough shape on the drawing, and then may change the size or add details on the basis of the drawing. The advantage is that it can be modified without cost, which is more intuitive.
(2) 3D modeling by design software
When considering how the parts are assembled, designers use modeling software to model, simulate the assembly process, play a role in pre-assembly, and verify whether this method is feasible.
(3) Use computer software to make animations to show the general working principles and functions
After the function is designed, a vivid effect can be made through animation demonstration, and check whether it is in line with expectations and whether it can be achieved
(4) Role play
When verifying the flexibility of the steering wheels, Dyson employees dressed up as users walking through narrowing roadblocks to verify whether the flexibility could meet the requirements.
(5) Using cardboard and plastics to make mockup
And when some structures are determined, some simple materials can be used to build these structures to verify whether the relevant functions can be achieved

Why failures are important

As James Dyson said, failure is a good starting point. Because when something fails, so that we know exactly what the problem is, and then know the reason for the failure in the experiment, engineers can use mathematics and scientific principles to analyze and improve, until the final solution is found.
As far as I am concerned, I think failure is the only way to make a product. If there is no failure, it will inevitably be reflected in the final product, and there will be many problems and mistakes that were not there before. Through failure, the correctness of the idea and the feasibility of the design can be tested, and the risk of the final product can be reduced.

Why math and science are important

The foundation of successful engineering design and Dyson’s basic principles is mathematics and science. Most of the work done by engineers includes mathematics and science. Trigonometric functions consider the relationship with other devices.
Use mathematics to calculate the percentage efficiency of dust to adjust the design on the tool to ensure that the maximum efficiency is achieved in the lowest level of mathematics. And science affects all areas of engineering. Both will speed up the process, bring engineers closer to the final result, and learn from it.

What’s the differences of view and approach between James Dyson and scientist? Why does it contribute to innovation?

Scientists tend to think right and it is difficult to think wrong, so they will not do something that seems stupid and wrong, but do something that is beneficial to them; James Dyson thinks he is an artist, he understands invention The value of, will start from another angle, so he will often try some wrong roads, or even completely wrong roads, he thinks this is valuable and meaningful, and will learn things in the process.
James Dyson’s views and methods are obviously beneficial to innovation, because innovation itself means a process from scratch. How many ideas were recognized as wrong or even disgusted at first, but they were finally proved by practice. Innovative products derived from this idea are valuable, so if you dare to take the wrong path and do the wrong thing, you can accelerate the process of innovation. At the same time, the wrong idea itself can also eliminate some mistakes and gain some experience, so that in the final product design process, you can avoid detours and come up with solutions faster.
